# LinkedIn Event Posts

Event posts cover announcements, recaps, and conference clusters. They drive attendance, build community presence, and create FOMO.

---

## Format 1: Event Announcement

**When to use:** You are speaking at, hosting, or attending an event and want to drive awareness or attendance.

**Structure:**
```
[What's happening + why it matters]

[Details: date, location, topic]

[Why the audience should care / what they'll learn]

[CTA: register, save the date, see you there]
```

**Template:**
```
[City] - [Date]: I'll be at [Event Name] talking about [topic].

The session is about [one sentence on the core question or problem].

If you're working on [audience problem], this is for you. We'll cover:
- [Takeaway 1]
- [Takeaway 2]
- [Takeaway 3]

[Number] spots left. Link in comments.

#event #topic #city
```

**Notes:**
- Lead with the city and date for scanability
- Focus on what the audience gets, not on you
- Always put the registration link in comments (better for algorithm)
- Tag the event organizer and co-speakers

---

## Format 2: Event Recap

**When to use:** You just spoke at, attended, or hosted an event and want to share takeaways.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook: the single best moment or insight from the event]

[Context: what the event was, who was there]

[2-3 specific takeaways or observations]

[Forward-looking: what you'll do differently, or what's next]
```

**Template:**
```
Best thing I heard at [Event Name] this week:

"[One specific quote or observation - keep under 15 words]"

[Why this resonated - 2-3 sentences]

Three other things I took away:

1. [Specific observation about the audience or talks]
2. [Something surprising or counterintuitive]
3. [A connection or opportunity that emerged]

Thanks to [organizer] for putting this together. Next up: [what's coming].

#event #topic #city
```

**Notes:**
- Lead with a specific moment, not a generic "great event" statement
- Name people - tag speakers, organizers, people you met
- Include a photo if possible (mention in the draft notes)
- Never open with "Had a blast at..." or "What an amazing event"

---

## Format 3: Conference Cluster

**When to use:** Attending a multi-day conference and posting daily updates, or clustering multiple related events.

**Structure:**
```
[Day X at Conference - the headline insight]

[What happened today]

[The non-obvious thing you noticed]

[What you're watching for tomorrow / next]
```

**Template:**
```
[Conference Name] Day [X]:

The hallway conversations are all about [topic].

[2-3 sentences on what you observed or learned today]

The pattern I'm seeing: [one insight that connects multiple talks or conversations]

Tomorrow I'm looking at [session/topic]. If you're here, find me at [location/time].

#conference #topic
```

**Notes:**
- Daily posts build narrative momentum - each should stand alone but connect
- Hallway observations often outperform talk summaries
- End each day's post with a hook for tomorrow
- Keep conference cluster posts shorter (100-150 words) since you're posting daily
