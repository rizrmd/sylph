# LinkedIn Ideas Posts

Ideas posts share opinions, benchmarks, and article commentary. They build authority and drive discussion.

---

## Format 1: Contrarian Take

**When to use:** You disagree with conventional wisdom or a popular opinion in your industry.

**Structure:**
```
[Bold claim that challenges the status quo]

[Why most people believe the opposite]

[Your evidence or experience that says otherwise]

[The nuance - when the conventional wisdom IS right]

[Invitation to disagree]
```

**Template:**
```
[Popular belief] is wrong.

Here's why everyone says it: [1-2 sentences on the conventional wisdom]

Here's what I've seen: [your counter-evidence - specific data, experience, or example]

[2-3 sentences expanding on why this matters]

The nuance: [when the popular belief actually holds - shows you're not just being contrarian for clicks]

What's your take? Have you seen the same thing?

#topic #industry
```

**Notes:**
- The claim must be genuinely held, not manufactured controversy
- Always include the nuance - pure contrarianism without nuance feels cheap
- Back it up with evidence, not just opinion
- End with a genuine question to drive comments
- This format gets high engagement but also attracts disagreement. That is good.

## Format 2: Benchmark / Data Share

**When to use:** You have data, benchmarks, or research findings worth sharing - either your own or from a credible source.

**Structure:**
```
[The headline number or finding]

[Context: where this data comes from]

[Why it matters - the implications]

[What to do about it / how to interpret it]
```

**Template:**
```
[Specific number or finding].

We [ran/analyzed/surveyed] [what] across [sample size/scope].

The breakdown:
- [Data point 1]: [what it means]
- [Data point 2]: [what it means]
- [Data point 3]: [what it means]

What surprised me: [the non-obvious finding]

If you're a [role], this means [actionable implication].

Full data in [location]. Link in comments.

#data #benchmark #topic
```

**Notes:**
- Lead with the most surprising or actionable number
- Always cite the source and sample size
- Distinguish between your data and third-party data
- Actionable implications beat raw numbers
- If sharing someone else's research, add your own interpretation

## Format 3: Article Share / Commentary

**When to use:** Sharing and commenting on an article, blog post, video, or external content.

**Structure:**
```
[Your take on the content - not a summary]

[The one thing that stood out most]

[How this connects to your experience or industry]

[Credit the author + link]
```

**Template:**
```
[Author] wrote something worth reading about [topic].

The key insight: [one sentence - your interpretation, not a quote]

This matches what I've been seeing: [your experience or data that validates/challenges the article]

[1-2 sentences on the broader implication]

Where I'd push back: [one point of disagreement or extension - optional but adds value]

Read the full piece - link in comments. Credit to [author/publication].

#topic #reading
```

**Notes:**
- Never just share a link with "Great article!" - add your perspective
- Your commentary should be longer than your summary of the article
- Always credit the author by name and tag them
- Add where you agree AND where you'd push back
- Fetch the article content before writing so you can reference specific points
- Respect copyright - don't reproduce large chunks of the article
