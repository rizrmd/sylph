## Voice Characteristics

### Tone Spectrum

| Attribute | We ARE | We are NOT |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Confident | Direct, assured, expert | Arrogant, dismissive |
| Technical | Precise, specific, practitioner-level | Jargon-heavy, gatekeeping |
| Approachable | Warm, clear, human | Casual, slangy, try-hard |
| Modern | Current, forward-looking | Trendy, hype-driven |
| Concise | Every word earns its place | Terse, cold, incomplete |

## Copy Formats

### Headlines
- Short. 2-5 words ideal.
- Can break grammar rules for impact (fragments OK)
- Use heading font in visual contexts

**Examples:**
- [example headline 1]
- [example headline 2]
- [example headline 3]

### Product Descriptions
- Lead with what it does, not what it is
- One sentence = one idea
- End with the benefit, not the feature

**Pattern:** [Action verb] your [thing] - [benefit].

### Feature Copy
- Start with the verb
- Stay under 15 words per feature line
- No periods at the end of feature list items (unless they're full sentences)

### Social Media
- LinkedIn: professional but not stiff. Share insights, not announcements.
- Twitter/X: sharper, punchier. Fragment sentences OK. Personality welcome.
- Exclamation marks: sparingly, max one per post.

### Email
- Subject lines: clear and specific, never clickbait
- Body: conversational but purposeful
- CTA: one clear action per email
